Why I built this

"When I'm reciting and my brain slips from one verse to a similar one, I want a tool that captures that relationship. So next time I encounter it, I can immediately recall: ah, this is the one that sounds like 60:9. The difference is here."

Mutashabihat are verses nearly identical in wording, and among the hardest parts of Quran memorization to master. Standard review tools don't model this kind of confusion. hifzlink does.

{{/* ── Features ───────────────────────────────────────────── */}}

Features

Everything you need for mutashabihat review

Word-level diff

Compare two similar verses side by side with word-level highlighting. See precisely where one verse diverges from the other.

Search by ayah or surah

Look up any ayah reference, surah number, or surah name to instantly see all saved pairs involving it.

Browse by Surah & Juz

Explore the full map of known similar verses organized by surah or juz. Scan where confusions are concentrated.

Collections

Group pairs into named collections for focused review sessions. Isolate the verses you're currently working on. Login required.

Verse audio

Listen to any verse with Alafasy recitation, powered by the Quran Foundation. Available on every ayah detail page.

How it works

Three steps from confusion to clarity

  1. 1

    Encounter a confusion

    During recitation, your mind slips. You land on the wrong verse or can't tell two similar ones apart.

  2. 2

    Search for the pair

    Type the ayah reference into Search. If the pair is saved, you'll see it immediately, along with any other verses that share the same confusion pattern.

  3. 3

    Compare and lock it in

    Open the side-by-side compare view. The highlighted diff shows exactly which words differ, so your brain builds the right anchor.
